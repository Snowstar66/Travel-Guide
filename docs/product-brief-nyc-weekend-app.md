# Product Brief: Weekend Guide for First-Time NYC Visitors

## Working Title

Trip Companion NYC

## One-Sentence Vision

An app that helps first-time visitors feel excited, oriented, and safe in New York City through a readable five-day weekend guide with practical day-by-day support.

## Problem

First-time visitors to New York often feel one or more of these at the same time:

- overwhelmed by the size of the city
- unsure what is actually worth doing on a short trip
- anxious about transport, neighborhoods, timing, and tourist traps
- stuck between boring itinerary apps and chaotic travel blogs

Most travel tools help users collect places. Few help them understand how to move through a city with confidence.

## Product Promise

The app should feel good enough to read on the flight, calm enough to trust after landing, and useful enough to keep open during the trip.

## Primary User

A first-time tourist visiting New York for about five days who wants:

- a clear plan without overplanning
- iconic experiences plus local-feeling moments
- help navigating safely and confidently
- a way to save thoughts, favorites, and progress during the trip

## Core Jobs To Be Done

When I am visiting New York for the first time:

1. Help me understand what kind of city I am entering before I land.
2. Tell me what I should do each day without making me feel trapped by a rigid itinerary.
3. Make transport and neighborhood decisions feel easy.
4. Warn me about common mistakes and energy drains.
5. Let me check off moments and write quick notes so the trip feels personal.

## Product Principles

1. Readable before usable.
   The content must be compelling enough to read like a travel companion, not just a utility app.

2. Calm over clutter.
   Reduce choice overload. Offer a strong default path with lightweight alternatives.

3. Confidence over completeness.
   Teach users how to handle the city, not just where to go.

4. Flexible structure.
   Every day should have a recommended flow plus a lighter and more ambitious version.

5. Memory matters.
   Small notes, saved favorites, and completed moments should make the trip feel owned by the traveler.

## MVP Scope

### Included in V1

- One city: New York City
- One audience: first-time visitors
- One trip length: five-day trip
- Day-by-day guide for Day 1 to Day 5
- Each day split into morning, afternoon, evening
- Calm practical guidance:
  - what to do
  - why this order works
  - how to get around
  - what to watch out for
  - what to eat nearby
- Check-off functionality for stops and moments
- Personal notes per day and per stop
- Favorites or saved highlights
- “Plan B” suggestions for rain, fatigue, or long lines
- Short orientation content:
  - borough basics
  - subway basics
  - first-time visitor safety tips
  - common scams or friction points

### Explicitly Not in V1

- bookings
- live navigation
- social features
- multiple cities
- personalized AI itinerary generation
- deep map layer
- full offline maps

## Content Structure

### Intro Layer

- Welcome to New York
- What first-time visitors usually get wrong
- How the city is laid out
- How to use the subway without stress
- What to book in advance

### Daily Layer

Each day should include:

- a mood or theme
- a recommended route
- 3 to 5 anchor stops
- 1 to 2 optional swaps
- food cue
- safety or timing tip
- energy level guidance

### Companion Layer

- quick notes
- completed items
- saved places
- “if you only do one thing here” prompts

## Example Five-Day Structure

### Day 1: Arrive and Get Your Bearings

- hotel zone orientation
- subway confidence starter
- first iconic walk
- easy dinner with low decision stress

### Day 2: Classic Manhattan

- Midtown essentials
- one major viewpoint or landmark
- low-friction first full sightseeing day

### Day 3: Downtown and City History

- Lower Manhattan
- ferry or skyline experience
- city-story layer

### Day 4: Neighborhood New York

- Village, SoHo, Brooklyn, or similar areas
- slower pace
- local-feeling discoveries

### Day 5: Choose Your Ending

- culture track
- food and shopping track
- relaxed final day track

## Information Architecture

Top-level app sections:

1. Home
2. Your 5-Day Trip
3. Today
4. Saved
5. Notes
6. Know Before You Go

## Core Screens

1. Onboarding
   Capture trip dates, arrival airport, hotel area, travel style, and energy preference.

2. Home
   Show trip countdown or current day, today’s plan, and one recommended next action.

3. Day Overview
   Show morning, afternoon, evening, optional swaps, and practical tips.

4. Stop Detail
   Explain why the stop matters, how long it takes, nearby food, and what to watch out for.

5. Notes
   Let the user write quick free-text notes tied to a day or place.

6. Saved
   Collect favorites and unfinished ideas.

7. Know Before You Go
   Lightweight guide to transit, safety, neighborhoods, and booking advice.

## Differentiators

- written like a smart travel companion instead of a spreadsheet
- optimized for first-time confidence, not just attraction discovery
- combines narrative, practical movement, and memory capture
- structured enough to reduce anxiety but flexible enough to feel human

## Success Criteria for MVP

The MVP is successful if a first-time visitor can:

- read the intro and feel more excited than anxious
- follow each day without needing to research from scratch
- use check-off and notes during the trip
- finish the trip feeling guided rather than managed
